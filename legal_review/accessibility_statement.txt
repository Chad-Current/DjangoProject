ACCESSIBILITY STATEMENT
Novatern
Last Updated: February 27, 2026
================================================================================


1. OUR COMMITMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Novatern is committed to providing a website and services that are accessible
to the widest possible audience, including people with disabilities. We aim to
follow recognized accessibility standards so that families, seniors, and helpers
can use our resources with confidence.


2. STANDARDS AND GUIDELINES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
We strive to design and develop our website in accordance with:

  - The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1, aiming for Level AA
    where reasonably possible.
  - Best practices for readable typography, clear structure, and
    keyboard-friendly navigation.

NOTE: Accessibility is an ongoing effort, and we continuously review our site
as we add or update content and features.


3. ACCESSIBILITY FEATURES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
We work to support:

Keyboard Navigation
  Full keyboard access for menus, links, and forms.

Color Contrast
  Sufficient contrast between text and background colors to aid readability.

Clear Structure
  Logical headings, descriptive labels, and clear instructions on all forms.

Responsive Layouts
  Optimized for desktop, tablet, and mobile devices.

Meaningful Link Text
  Descriptive link text instead of generic wording like "click here."

NOTE: Where we use PDFs or downloadable documents, we aim to make them
screen-reader friendly whenever possible.


4. SUPPORTED TECHNOLOGIES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Our website is intended to work with:

  - Current versions of major browsers (such as Chrome, Edge, Firefox, and
    Safari).
  - Common assistive technologies, including screen readers and built-in
    browser zoom.

NOTE: For the best experience, we recommend using the latest version of your
browser and operating system.


5. KNOWN LIMITATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Despite our efforts, some content or older documents may not yet be fully
accessible. Examples may include:

  - Legacy PDFs or downloads that are not fully tagged for screen readers.
  - Third-party plugins, widgets, or embedded tools we do not control.

NOTE: When we identify accessibility issues, we prioritize fixing them in
future updates.


6. FEEDBACK AND SUPPORT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
We welcome feedback on the accessibility of Novatern. If you encounter any
barriers or have difficulty using any part of the site, please let us know so
we can assist you and improve.

Please contact us with:
  - A description of the issue.
  - The page or feature where you experienced the problem.
  - The browser and assistive technology (if any) you were using.

  Novatern
  Accessibility: accessibility@novatern.com
  Alternate email: privacy@novatern.com
  Location: Des Moines, Iowa, USA

We aim to respond to accessibility inquiries within a reasonable timeframe and,
where possible, offer an accessible alternative or direct support.


7. ONGOING IMPROVEMENTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Accessibility is not a one-time project but an ongoing process. We
periodically:

  - Review new pages and features for accessibility.
  - Adjust design and code based on feedback and updated standards.
  - Consider the needs of seniors, caregivers, and people with varying levels
    of digital experience when designing our content.
