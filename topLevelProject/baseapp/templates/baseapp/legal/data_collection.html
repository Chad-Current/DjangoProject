Data Collection Policy

Last Updated: April 13, 2026

1. Introduction

At Novatern ("we," "our," or "us"), your privacy and data security are our highest priorities. This Data Collection Policy explains what information we collect, how we collect it, and how it is used and protected in the course of delivering our digital estate organization services.

This policy applies to all information collected through our website, authenticated user sessions, and any secure storage systems we operate.

2. Information We Collect

We collect information only as needed to provide, secure, and improve our services. The types of data we may collect include:

Personal Identification Data Name, email address, username, phone number, and general contact information voluntarily provided by you during registration or profile setup.
Estate Planning Records Digital account records, device information, contacts, estate documents, funeral preferences, and other information you enter while using the dashboard.
Encrypted Vault Credentials Passwords or PINs stored in the Password Vault are encrypted with AES-256 (Fernet) before being written to the database and are never persisted in plaintext. Login identifiers (username or email) linked to vault entries are stored in unencrypted form alongside the encrypted password token. Financial institution account passwords cannot be stored and are blocked at both the interface and server level.
Vault Access Logs An immutable audit log is created each time a vault password is revealed. The log records the user account, date and time, and IP address of the request. These logs are retained for security and accountability purposes and cannot be deleted by users.
Uploaded Files Files uploaded as part of your estate records (e.g., document scans) are stored on our servers. Access is restricted to your authenticated account and any recovery access you have authorized.
Technical Data Browser type, IP address, device information, and website activity (collected via analytics) to enhance site performance and reliability.
Communication Data Correspondence or support requests sent to us by email or contact form.
We do not collect or store full Social Security numbers, full financial account numbers, routing numbers, or CVV codes. We do not sell or rent any collected data to third parties.
3. How Information Is Collected

We collect data in several ways:

  • Directly from you when you register, update your profile, enter estate planning data, or upload files.
  • Automatically through session management, essential cookies, analytics tools, and server logs that monitor website performance.
  • Through Stripe for payment processing — we receive confirmation of payment events but do not store card details on our servers.
4. How We Use Collected Data

Your information may be used to:

  • Provide the digital estate organization platform, including all dashboard features, across all tiers (Free, Essentials, and Legacy).
  • Manage subscription tiers and enforce per-category item limits and access controls appropriate to your tier.
  • Facilitate family recovery access requests upon administrative review and verification.
  • Maintain vault access audit logs for security and accountability.
  • Facilitate communication and support.
  • Improve our website, services, and security infrastructure.
  • Comply with applicable laws and regulations.
We do not use personal data for marketing or profiling purposes unless you expressly opt in.
5. Data Storage and Security

We use encrypted and access-controlled systems to keep your data safe.

Vault Encryption Vault passwords are encrypted using AES-256 (Fernet symmetric encryption). Encryption keys are stored in server environment variables, not in the database. Decryption occurs only on explicit user request within an authenticated session and is logged automatically.
Access Control Features are gated by subscription tier and enforced at the server level. Sessions expire after 1 hour of inactivity. Repeated failed login attempts trigger a temporary account lockout.
Financial Account Restriction Passwords for financial institution accounts (Online Banking, Neobank/Digital Bank, Brokerage/Investment, Cryptocurrency Exchange, Payment Processor, Payment Wallet) cannot be stored in the vault. This restriction is enforced server-side and cannot be bypassed.
User Risk Acknowledgment Before storing any credential in the Password Vault, users must affirmatively acknowledge the inherent risk of storing credentials online. This acknowledgment is required at the time of each vault entry creation.
6. Data Retention

We retain information only as long as necessary to fulfill service obligations or meet legal and regulatory requirements. Vault access logs are retained for the duration of the account and for a period thereafter for security accountability. You may request permanent deletion of your personal information or uploaded data at any time by contacting  privacy@novatern.com . See our Data Retention Policy for specific periods.

7. Sharing and Disclosure

We may share limited data only:

  • With your explicit consent, including when a family recovery request is approved.
  • With infrastructure service providers (e.g., hosting and payment processors) under strict confidentiality agreements.
  • When required by law, court order, or regulatory authority.
We will never sell, lease, or trade your information with outside organizations for any purpose.
8. Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have the right to:

  • Request access to your personal data.
  • Request correction or deletion of your data.
  • Withdraw consent at any time.
  • Request a summary of data stored about you.

Contact  privacy@novatern.com  to exercise any of these rights.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Data Collection Policy periodically to reflect updates in laws, technology, or business practices. All revisions will be posted here with an updated "Last Updated" date.
