Accessibility Statement

Last Updated: February 27, 2026

1. Our Commitment

Digital Estate Plan is committed to providing a website and services that are accessible to the widest possible audience, including people with disabilities. We aim to follow recognized accessibility standards so that families, seniors, and helpers can use our resources with confidence.

2. Standards and Guidelines

We strive to design and develop our website in accordance with:

  • The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1, aiming for Level AA where reasonably possible.
  • Best practices for readable typography, clear structure, and keyboard-friendly navigation.
Accessibility is an ongoing effort, and we continuously review our site as we add or update content and features.
3. Accessibility Features

We work to support:

Keyboard Navigation Full keyboard access for menus, links, and forms.
Color Contrast Sufficient contrast between text and background colors to aid readability.
Clear Structure Logical headings, descriptive labels, and clear instructions on all forms.
Responsive Layouts Optimized for desktop, tablet, and mobile devices.
Meaningful Link Text Descriptive link text instead of generic wording like "click here."
Where we use PDFs or downloadable documents, we aim to make them screen-reader friendly whenever possible.
4. Supported Technologies

Our website is intended to work with:

  • Current versions of major browsers (such as Chrome, Edge, Firefox, and Safari).
  • Common assistive technologies, including screen readers and built-in browser zoom.
For the best experience, we recommend using the latest version of your browser and operating system.
5. Known Limitations

Despite our efforts, some content or older documents may not yet be fully accessible. Examples may include:

  • Legacy PDFs or downloads that are not fully tagged for screen readers.
  • Third-party plugins, widgets, or embedded tools we do not control.
When we identify accessibility issues, we prioritize fixing them in future updates.
6. Feedback and Support

We welcome feedback on the accessibility of Digital Estate Plan. If you encounter any barriers or have difficulty using any part of the site, please let us know so we can assist you and improve.

Please contact us with:

  • A description of the issue.
  • The page or feature where you experienced the problem.
  • The browser and assistive technology (if any) you were using.

7. Ongoing Improvements

Accessibility is not a one-time project but an ongoing process. We periodically:

  • Review new pages and features for accessibility.
  • Adjust design and code based on feedback and updated standards.
  • Consider the needs of seniors, caregivers, and people with varying levels of digital experience when designing our content.
