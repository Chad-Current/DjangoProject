Data Retention Policy

Last Updated: February 27, 2026

1. Purpose and Scope

This Data Retention Policy explains how Digital Estate Plan ("we," "our," or "us") retains, archives, and deletes personal and client information collected through our website, services, and secure storage systems.

It applies to all digital records, uploaded files, support communications, and limited technical data we handle in the course of providing digital estate organization services.

2. Principles of Retention

We follow these core principles when retaining data:

  • Keep data only as long as necessary for the purpose it was collected or as required by law.
  • Minimize the amount of personal information stored and avoid indefinite retention.
  • Apply appropriate security controls to all retained data.
  • Delete or anonymize data once it is no longer needed.
3. Data Categories and Retention Periods

Unless a different period is required by law or agreed with you in writing, we generally apply the following retention periods:

Account and Contact Information Name, email, phone, and basic profile details. Retained for up to 3 years after the last meaningful interaction (login, service use, or direct contact), then deleted or anonymized.
Client Project and Service Records Notes related to your digital organization project, configuration details, and non-legal documentation. Retained for up to 5 years after the completion of services, unless you request earlier deletion.
Uploaded Files and Documents Files stored with us for organizational purposes. Retained for the duration of the active service and up to 1 year after service completion, unless you request immediate deletion or extended retention.
Support and Communication Records Email, messages, and service notes. Retained for up to 3 years from the date of the last communication, primarily for service history and dispute resolution.
Technical and Analytics Data IP address, device type, usage logs, and analytics. Retained in identifiable form for up to 12 months, after which it may be aggregated or anonymized for long-term trend analysis.
If applicable law requires a longer retention period (for example, tax or business records), we will retain the relevant records for the legally required period.
4. Archiving

When data is no longer actively used but must be retained for legal, security, or limited business reasons, we may move it into a secure archive.

  • Archived data is stored in encrypted environments with strict access controls.
  • Access to archived data is restricted to authorized personnel only and used solely for compliance, security, or documented business needs.
5. Deletion and Disposal

Once a retention period expires and no legal or operational reason remains to keep the data, we will securely delete or anonymize it.

Electronic Records Permanently deleted from active systems and, where technically feasible, from backups according to our backup rotation schedule.
Anonymization In some cases, data may be anonymized so it can no longer be linked to an identifiable person, and may be retained for statistical or planning purposes.
6. Legal Holds and Exceptions

If we become aware of a legal claim, investigation, audit, or other legal requirement relating to specific data, we may place a "legal hold" on that data.

  • During a legal hold, affected data will not be deleted — even if its normal retention period has expired — until the hold is lifted.
  • Once the legal hold ends, normal retention and deletion rules will resume.
7. Your Rights and Requests for Deletion

You may request access to, correction of, or deletion of your personal data at any time, subject to legal and contractual limitations.

We will honor verified requests to delete personal and uploaded data unless we are required to keep some records for legal, regulatory, or security reasons.

To submit a request, contact us at  privacy@digitalestateplan.com  with sufficient information to verify your identity and identify the records in question.

8. Responsibilities

We maintain internal roles and responsibilities to support proper retention and disposal:

Management / Owners Define retention rules and ensure this policy aligns with legal requirements and business needs.
Technical / IT Support Implement secure storage, archiving, and deletion procedures in line with this policy.
All Staff and Contractors Handle data according to this policy and avoid creating or retaining unnecessary copies of personal information.
9. Updates to This Policy

We may update this Data Retention Policy periodically to reflect changes in laws, technology, or our services. Any updates will be posted on our website with an updated "Last Updated" date, and continued use of our services signifies acceptance of the revised policy.
