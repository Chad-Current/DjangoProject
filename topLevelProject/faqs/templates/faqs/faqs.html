Security & Privacy 4

How do I know my information is safe?

Your privacy and security are taken seriously at every step of the process.

  • Mail-only intake: All documents are sent using a prepaid USPS Priority Mail envelope with tracking — no email, no uploads, no online portals.
  • Single-handler process: Your documents are handled by one person only. No assistants, contractors, or third-party scanning services.
  • Private handling: Documents are opened and processed in a private, controlled workspace.
  • No originals kept: Send copies only. If an original is accidentally included, it is not scanned and is returned immediately.
Who can see my passwords and stored credentials?

Only you. We do not have access to any passwords or sensitive credentials stored in your account. All sensitive data is encrypted and accessible only to the account owner and contacts you explicitly delegate access to.

What happens to my data when I pass away?

Your designated Digital Executor and delegated contacts will be granted access to the items you've assigned to them. Only the information you've explicitly shared will be visible — nothing more. Your account remains otherwise private and protected.

What if I forget my master password?

Use the Forgot Password link on the login page. A reset link will be sent to your registered email address. If you no longer have access to that email, contact us directly for identity-verified account recovery.

Process & Timeline 4

How do I get started?

Getting started takes just a few steps:

  • Create your account and complete your profile.
  • Add your contacts and assign roles (Emergency Contact, Digital Executor, Caregiver).
  • Begin adding accounts, devices, and documents, delegating each to a contact.
  • Review your dashboard regularly to keep everything up to date.
How does the mail-in document process work?

Once you sign up, you'll receive a prepaid USPS Priority Mail envelope. Mail in copies of your documents — never originals. Documents are processed by a single handler, digitized, and linked to your account within 5–7 business days. You'll receive a confirmation email once complete.

How often should I update my information?

We recommend reviewing your account at least once a year or after any major life event — a move, new account, change in relationship, or updated legal document. Your dashboard shows the last updated date on every item to help you stay current.

How will my contacts be notified when the time comes?

Your Digital Executor is responsible for notifying your contacts and initiating the access process. We provide a clear, step-by-step guide your executor can follow. Each delegated contact will only see the specific items assigned to them — nothing else in your account.

Technical Questions 4

What devices and browsers are supported?

The platform is fully responsive and works on desktop, tablet, and mobile devices. We support all modern browsers including Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge. For the best experience, keep your browser updated to its latest version.

What file types can I upload to my documents?

You can upload PDFs, JPEGs, and PNGs for estate documents and important documents. Files are stored securely and accessible only to you and your delegated contacts. Individual file size is limited to 10MB.

Does the platform support two-factor authentication?

Yes. Two-factor authentication (2FA) is available and strongly recommended for all accounts. You can enable it in your account settings. When flagging devices in your dashboard, you can also note which device is used for 2FA so your executor knows to locate it.

Can I delete my account and all my data?

Yes. You can request full account deletion from your account settings. All stored data, documents, and delegations will be permanently removed within 30 days. This action is irreversible, so we recommend exporting any information you wish to keep beforehand.
