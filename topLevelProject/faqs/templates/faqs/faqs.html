Help & FAQs

Common questions, plain answers.

Your Security & Privacy
Is my personal information safe on this website? Show answer Hide answer

Yes. We've made security a foundation of the platform, not an afterthought. Here's what's in place:

  • All stored credentials and sensitive data are encrypted using Fernet symmetric encryption before they ever touch our database. Your data is never stored in plaintext.
  • Account access is protected with session-based authentication and automatic session timeouts.
  • Repeated failed login attempts trigger an account lockout to prevent unauthorized access.
  • The platform enforces standard protections against XSS, CSRF, and clickjacking on every page.
Can anyone else see my stored passwords and credentials? Show answer Hide answer

No. Stored credentials are encrypted and only decrypted on your explicit request. We cannot read them.

The only people who can access your vault entries are the trusted contacts you specifically designate — and only for the items you choose to share with them. Nothing is shared automatically or by default.

What is the Password Vault and how does it work? Show answer Hide answer

The Password Vault is a secure, encrypted storage space for the credentials tied to your digital accounts and devices — available exclusively on the Legacy plan.

Every credential you save is encrypted with Fernet symmetric encryption before it's stored. It is never saved in plaintext, and we cannot read it. You can only reveal a stored password by explicitly requesting it while logged in.

Every time a vault entry is revealed, the access is recorded in an audit log — so you have a full history of when credentials were viewed and by whom.

What if I forget my password to log in? Show answer Hide answer

No problem. On the login page, click reset password and we'll send a reset link to your email address.

If you no longer have access to that email address, contact us directly and we'll help you recover your account safely.

Your Family After You're Gone
Who will be able to see my information when I pass away? Show answer Hide answer

Only the people you choose. You decide exactly which trusted contacts can see which items — a specific bank account, a device, a document — and nothing else.

Family members who aren't designated contacts can submit a verified recovery request through our review process. Requests are only approved after proper documentation is provided — your information is never handed over without verification.

How will my family know what to do? Show answer Hide answer

You designate trusted contacts and assign each of them to specific accounts, devices, or documents. When the time comes, they know exactly what they're responsible for — and what you wanted done with it.

You can also add a personal note to each contact with any special instructions or context that would help them. Think of it as a private letter attached to each item.

Can I leave personal notes or instructions for specific people? Show answer Hide answer

Yes. When you add a contact, there is a field for personal notes or special instructions just for that person.

This is a good place to write things like: "Please cancel this subscription first," or "The hard drive in this laptop has photos you'll want to save." Whatever context would make their job easier.

Plans & Subscriptions
What does the free tier include? Show answer Hide answer

Free accounts let you explore every section of the platform and save a limited number of items in each category — no credit card required. You can add:

  • Up to 2 trusted contacts
  • 1 digital account & 1 device
  • 1 estate document & 1 important document
  • Up to 2 family notes

You can view everything you've saved at any time. When you're ready to add more, you can upgrade to Essentials or Legacy.

What's the difference between Essentials and Legacy? Show answer Hide answer

Both plans give you full edit access while your subscription is active. The key differences are:

  • Essentials ($5.99/mo or $59.99/yr) — full edit access with per-category limits: up to 3 contacts, 3 accounts, 3 devices, 3 estate docs, 5 important documents, and 5 family notes.
  • Legacy ($9.99/mo or $99.99/yr) — no item limits, plus exclusive access to the Funeral & Final Arrangements planning section and the encrypted Password Vault for securely storing credentials tied to your accounts and devices.

Both plans bill monthly or annually. Annual billing saves you two months compared to monthly.

How much does it cost? Show answer Hide answer

There are three options:

  • Free — $0, forever. No credit card required.
  • Essentials — $5.99/month, or $59.99/year (saves two months).
  • Legacy — $9.99/month, or $99.99/year (saves two months).

Subscriptions are managed through Stripe and can be canceled at any time. See our pricing page for a full feature comparison.

What happens to my data if I cancel? Show answer Hide answer

Your data stays in place. If you cancel a paid subscription, your account moves to limited view-only access. You won't be able to add or edit items until you resubscribe.

We don't delete your information when you cancel. The whole point of this platform is that your plan should outlast any billing cycle.

Your designated digital executors (contacts) can request access to your data in the occurance of an unfortunate life event.

Using This Website
How do I get started? Show answer Hide answer

After you create your account and set up your profile, we walk you through a step-by-step onboarding flow that covers each section of your estate plan in order — contacts, accounts, devices, estate documents, important documents, and family notes. You can skip any step and come back later.

If you prefer to explore on your own, your dashboard gives you direct access to every section. Most people find this order works well:

  • Start with your Trusted Contacts — add the people you want to have access to your information.
  • Then add your Accounts, Devices, and Documents one at a time.
  • For each item, assign which contact should handle it and add any notes that would help them.
  • You can also download our free checklist guide as a starting point if you're not sure what to include.
How often do I need to update my information? Show answer Hide answer

We recommend a quick review once a year — your birthday or the new year are easy anchors. It's also worth updating after any major life change:

  • Opening or closing a bank account or subscription
  • Getting a new phone, computer, or device
  • A change in your family situation — marriage, divorce, a new child
  • Updating a will, power of attorney, or healthcare directive

Essentials and Legacy subscribers also have access to built-in relevance reviews — a tool to log when you last checked each item and set a reminder for the next review. It's a simple way to keep your plan current without having to remember on your own.

Can I use this on my phone or tablet? Show answer Hide answer

Yes. The website is fully responsive and works on phones, tablets, and computers. No app download required.
